Indian Foods

1. North Indian Cuisine (Wheat, Dairy, and Tandoor)

The food of North India, particularly the Mughlai and Punjabi styles, is globally the most recognized. It is characterized by its richness, use of wheat-based breads, yogurt, ghee, and paneer.

Butter Chicken

Chicken in a mildly spiced tomato, butter, and cream sauce.

Paneer Tikka

Marinated in yogurt and spices, cooked in a clay oven for smoky flavor.

Dal Makhani

Slow-cooked black lentils in butter and cream.

2. East Indian Cuisine (Rice, Fish, and Mustard Oil)

Cuisine from the East—Bengal, Odisha, and Bihar—focuses on rice and fish, and uses mustard oil for its pungent flavor. The region is also known for its sweets.

Machher Jhol

Fish curry with vegetables in a mustard-based broth.

Litti Chokha

Stuffed wheat balls with smoky mashed vegetables.

Rasgulla

Soft chhena (cheese) balls soaked in sugar syrup.

3. West Indian Cuisine (Sweet & Sour, Street Food, and Legumes)

Western Indian cuisine—Gujarat, Maharashtra, and Goa—ranges from vegetarian sweet-spicy snacks to coastal seafood. Known for street food and complex flavor balances.

Vada Pav

Spicy potato fritter inside a bread roll, Mumbai’s iconic snack.

Goan Fish Curry

Fish in spicy coconut milk curry with kokum or tamarind.

Falooda

Creamy frozen dessert with rose syrup and vermicelli.

4. South Indian Cuisine (Rice, Coconut, and Tempering)

South Indian food (Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Karnataka, Andhra/Telangana) revolves around rice and lentils, with unique tempering (mustard seeds, curry leaves, chilies, and urad dal).

Dosa

Fermented rice-lentil dishes served with sambar and chutneys.

Hyderabadi Biryani

Fragrant rice layered with meat and saffron, a royal delicacy.

Appam

Kerala pancake made with rice and coconut, served with gravy.
