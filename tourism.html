Heritage and Architecture

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a stunning white marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Renowned for its symmetrical beauty and intricate craftsmanship, it stands as a symbol of eternal love on the banks of the Yamuna River in Agra.

Hampi

Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for the majestic ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire, showcasing grand temples, stone chariots, and royal complexes. Set amidst boulder-strewn landscapes, it reflects the architectural and cultural brilliance of 14th-century South India.

Sun Temple

Sun Temple is located in Odisha, a 13th-century architectural marvel designed as a colossal chariot dedicated to the Sun God, with intricately carved stone wheels and sculptures. It represents the pinnacle of Kalinga architecture and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Tanjavur Temple

Tanjavur Temple, is a grand 11th-century temple built by Chola emperor Raja Raja Chola I in Tamil Nadu. it is renowned for its massive vimana (tower), intricate frescoes, and precision stone work, symbolizing the zenith of Chola architecture. It is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site, part of the "Great Living Chola Temples.

Nature and Wildlife

Valley of Flowers

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this alpine valley bursts into a riot of colors with blooming wildflowers during monsoon, surrounded by snow-capped peaks.

Sundarbans

The world’s largest mangrove forest and a UNESCO site, home to the famous Royal Bengal Tiger, unique flora, and a network of tidal waterways.

Munnar

A serene hill station in the Western Ghats, known for its tea gardens, misty hills, waterfalls, and rich biodiversity.

Jim Corbett National Park

It is located in Uttarakhand, is India’s oldest national park and a key part of Project Tiger, established in 1936. It is renowned for its rich biodiversity, including Bengal tigers, elephants, and over 600 species of birds.

Spiritual and Pilgrimage

Varanasi

One of the oldest living cities in the world, located on the banks of the Ganges, revered for its ghats, temples, and spiritual rituals.

Velankanni Church

Also known as the Basilica of Our Lady of Good Health, it's a major pilgrimage site attracting millions of devotees, especially during the annual festival in September.

Ajmer Sharif Dargah

The shrine of Sufi saint Khwaja Moinuddin Chishti, it is visited by people of all faiths seeking blessings and spiritual solace.

Golden Temple

The holiest shrine of Sikhism, known for its stunning golden architecture and the spirit of equality and community service through the langar (community kitchen).

Outdoor and Adventures

Rishikesh

Famous for white-water rafting on the Ganges, bungee jumping, and trekking, along with serene nature and yoga retreats.

Ladakh

A high-altitude desert ideal for trekking, mountain biking, and river rafting, with dramatic landscapes and eco-conscious travel practices.

Spiti Valley

A remote Himalayan destination known for high-altitude trekking, camping, and eco-friendly homestays amid stark, stunning terrain.

Andaman and Nicobar Islands

Offers scuba diving, snorkeling, and sea walking in pristine coral reefs, with a focus on marine conservation and eco-tourism.
